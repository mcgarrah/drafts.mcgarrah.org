SVP / Head of AI — Persona Positioning Plan

Strategic assessment of published posts (April 2026 – present) and near-ready drafts that can be repositioned to demonstrate readiness for SVP of Machine Learning or Head of AI Initiatives.

The goal: a hiring committee or board member reads across three channels — the blog, Substack, and LinkedIn — and sees someone who bridges enterprise architecture, AI/ML depth, security discipline, and business strategy. The “one-stop shopping” leader who can identify both the business case and the technology stack.

Created: 2026-07-30

Distribution Strategy: Three Channels, Three Audiences

The SVP persona needs to reach different audiences through different channels. Each channel has a distinct role.

Channel Role Audience Content Length
Blog (mcgarrah.org) Technical source of truth Engineers, architects, hiring managers doing deep diligence Full technical depth
Substack (mcgarrah.substack.com) Long-form leadership home Leadership audience, newsletter subscribers, persistent archive 1500-3000 words, strategic framing
LinkedIn Amplifier + hook 500+ connections: former colleagues, recruiters, hiring committees 300-800 words, drives to Substack

How the Channels Reinforce Each Other

  • Blog is the technical source of truth. Every hands-on article lives here permanently. This is where the “still builds things” credibility lives. When a hiring committee does deep technical diligence, this is what they find.
  • Substack is the long-form leadership home. The retrospective newsletters bundle blog posts with strategic framing. The standalone leadership articles live here as the persistent, linkable, shareable versions. Substack grows slowly but compounds — it’s the archive that outlasts any single LinkedIn post’s feed visibility. Substack is where the SVP-voiced content lives.
  • LinkedIn is the amplifier. Short posts (300-800 words) that distill the key insight from a Substack article into a bite-sized hook, then link to the full Substack piece. LinkedIn’s value is reach into your 500+ professional network — former colleagues who can vouch for the work, recruiters sourcing for SVP roles, and hiring managers evaluating candidates. LinkedIn posts are ephemeral in the feed; Substack articles are permanent.

The LinkedIn Advantage: Reach Into Your Network

With 500+ connections who are former colleagues, managers, and industry peers, LinkedIn is where the SVP persona has immediate reach. A Substack article that nobody reads doesn’t move the needle. But a LinkedIn post that hooks 3-5 former colleagues into reading the full Substack piece — and commenting “I saw Michael build this at [company]” — is worth more than 10,000 anonymous page views.

LinkedIn properties that matter for SVP positioning:

  • Social proof is visible. Colleague comments become public endorsements.
  • Recruiters live there. Executive recruiters sourcing for SVP/Head of AI search LinkedIn.
  • The network effect is immediate. Your connections see the post; their connections see their engagement.
  • Posts persist on your profile. LinkedIn articles/posts become part of your professional profile visible to anyone who views it.

What Needs Attention

Experience Descriptions Are IC-Voiced

The biggest gap is in the experience section. Every role description reads as what you did, not what you led, decided, or enabled. This is the same Pattern #1 from PERSONA.md (“Lead with Why, Not What”) applied to job descriptions.

Envestnet (current role):

“Building large scale cloud platforms for financial services and data science initiatives.”

This is IC language. “Building” = doing the work. For SVP positioning, reframe as:

“Leading cloud platform architecture for financial services, designing the infrastructure that supports data science initiatives across the organization. Driving automation-first strategy integrating AWS cloud and on-premises data center infrastructure.”

Blue Cross NC:

“Building a data science platform incorporating a data-warehouse, data-lake and data-execution environment”

Reframe to surface the leadership and impact:

“Architected and led development of the enterprise data science platform — integrating data warehouse, data lake, and containerized execution environment — that powered production deep learning models for healthcare predictions. Platform supported the CarePath initiative (ISSIP Excellence Award).”

USPS:

“Engaged in advanced analytics with multiple platforms.”

“Engaged in” is passive. Reframe:

“Administered a 25-node SAS Viya in-memory analytics cluster (26TB RAM) linked to a Hadoop data lake approaching 1PB. Operated in a high-security federal environment processing terabyte-scale datasets for advanced analytics and machine learning.”

NC DIT: This one is already the strongest — “Accountable for developing, maintaining and overseeing” is leadership language. Minor polish only.

NC DOR / ETAAC: These are already strong. The ETAAC description is excellent — “reports to Congress annually” is a credibility signal most candidates can’t match.

Missing: ISSIP Excellence Award

The CarePath deep learning work at BCBSNC won an ISSIP Excellence Award. This should be in the Blue Cross NC description and possibly in Honors & Awards.

Missing: BD Biosciences Title Undersells

The PDF says “IT Engineer” but the authorial-voice.md references “IT Director at BD Biosciences.” If the actual title was IT Director (or if you functioned as one — sole IT person for the plant), the LinkedIn title should reflect that. “Provide for all IT services at the BD Treyburn manufacturing plant” is director-level scope described with an engineer title.

Certifications Are Dated

The certifications are all 2002-2006 era (Windows 2000, Nortel Networks). They don’t hurt, but they don’t help for SVP positioning either. Consider:

  • Adding AWS certifications if you have them (or plan to get them)
  • The SAS certifications are still relevant for the data science narrative
  • The ITIL Foundation cert is fine

Early Career Consolidation

The pre-2005 roles (Interpath, Ziff-Davis, Pioneer Software, DB Basics, Roemer Weather, NC State TA/Library/Operator) add length without adding SVP signal. Consider:

  • Consolidating into a single “Early Career” entry: “1990-2005: Progressive roles in systems engineering, benchmark development, network operations, and technical services management across Ziff-Davis Publishing, Pioneer Software, NetIQ, NC State University, and others.”
  • This preserves the breadth signal without the recruiter scrolling through 8 pages.
